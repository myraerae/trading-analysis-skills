# Consolidation Breakout + Volume Spike — Crypto

## Definition

- **Consolidation**: Price trades in a narrow range (typically ±5–15%) for 7+ days (ideally 2–4 weeks). Low volatility, indecision.
- **Volume spike**: Trading volume suddenly 2–3x or more vs. 7d/14d average.
- **Breakout**: Price moves decisively above resistance or below support, with volume confirming the move.

## Why It Matters

- Consolidation often precedes major moves.
- Volume spike indicates institutional or large-player interest.
- Breakout + volume = higher-probability directional signal.

## Detection Checklist

1. **Range identification**: What is the consolidation range (high/low) and duration?
2. **Volume baseline**: 7d and 14d average volume.
3. **Volume spike**: Is current volume 2x+ the baseline?
4. **Breakout**: Did price close above resistance or below support?
5. **Confirmation**: Volume should expand on the breakout candle.

## False Breakout Warnings

- Breakout on low volume → suspect, wait for confirmation.
- Price quickly reverses back into range → false breakout.
- Single candle spike with no follow-through → cautious.

## Crypto-Specific Notes

- 24h markets: Use 7d/14d volume averages.
- High volatility: Wider consolidation ranges (e.g., ±10–20%) are normal.
- Correlations: BTC often leads; check if ETH/SOL follow or diverge.
