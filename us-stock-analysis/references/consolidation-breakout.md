# Consolidation Breakout + Volume Spike — US Stocks

## Definition

- **Consolidation**: Price trades in a narrow range (typically ±5–15%) for 2–4+ weeks. Low volatility, indecision.
- **Volume spike**: Trading volume suddenly 2–3x or more vs. 20-day average.
- **Breakout**: Price moves decisively above resistance or below support, with volume confirming the move.

## Why It Matters

- Consolidation often precedes major moves.
- Volume spike indicates institutional or large-player interest.
- Breakout + volume = higher-probability directional signal.

## Detection Checklist

1. **Range identification**: What is the consolidation range (high/low) and duration?
2. **Volume baseline**: 20-day average volume.
3. **Volume spike**: Is current volume 2x+ the 20d average?
4. **Breakout**: Did price close above resistance or below support?
5. **Confirmation**: Volume should expand on the breakout day.

## False Breakout Warnings

- Breakout on low volume → suspect, wait for confirmation.
- Price quickly reverses back into range → false breakout.
- Single-day spike with no follow-through → cautious.

## Market Cap Filter

- Only flag stocks with meaningful market cap ($2B+).
- Small-cap (<$2B) breakouts can be noisy and illiquid.
- Large-cap ($10B+) and mid-cap ($2B–$10B) preferred for recommendations.
